STUDY

For the first three years of my higher education, I was working on a BA degree in Kazan State University, Russia (now Kazan Federal University). Having moved to the USA during my 4th year, I decided to continue my education here. Once I was accepted to the University of Colorado Boulder, I fell in love with the school and the spirit of this university and wanted to pursue my education here. I majored in Anthropology and Linguistics during my undergraduate career, and in 2012 I enrolled into a concurrent BA/MA program in Linguistics. In 2014, I finished both of my undergraduates degrees with distinction and my Master's. For my MA thesis, I studied the ideologies surrounding language revitalization online. I analyzed several popular websites as tools of language learning and I observed the use of online materials in language classrooms of the Northern Arapaho in WY.

Today, I am a PhD student in Linguistics and I continue working in the Linguistic Anthropology path. I deeply interested in person reference in conversations and what it tells about social relationship of the speaker with their addressee and referent. Working with the Northern Arapaho language, I investigate not only the conversational practices, but also grammar. In the fall of 2015, I submitted my first qualifying research (aka "Prelim") on the changes in the use of obviatives in Arapaho. Today, I use methods of Conversation Analysis and Ethnography of Communication to research common conversational practices of this speech community. In 2017, I submitted my second qualifying paper (aka "Synthesis") discussing the variation in speech genres and person reference. I am planning to research speech genres and conversational sub-genres in my dissertation as an important analytical tool for studying community organization in a speech community.
