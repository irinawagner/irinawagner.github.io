IRINA WAGNER

I am a third year PhD student in the Department of Linguistics at the University of Colorado Boulder. My primary research interests include Native American languages, interactional linguistics, and language revitalization. For my dissertation, I am looking at person reference terms in the Arapaho (Algonquian) language. My preliminary research on the subject shows that Arapaho has a number of possible devices for referring to a third person which depend on the action being accomplished by the utterance. Eventually, I hope to make a connection between the person reference terminology and use and the assumed agency of the referred person.

The results of my work have been implemented in the development of the Arapaho Language Project and presented during the 46th Algonquian conference (2014).

Being interested in interdisciplinary research, I am currently pursuing two graduate certificates: Culture, Language and Social Practice and Native American and Indigenous Studies. I am also an active student and a research assistant at the Center of Indigenous Languages of the West.
